## Why Start a Blog?

I'm making a blog. Why am I doing this? There are many reasons it is an overtly foolish idea these days. To name a few...

- Writing on the internet is easily generated like never before, technical and otherwise
- Lots of tech people have a lot of reading to do. What makes what I have to say worth hearing? 
- All of my words will jsut get fed into the latest AI, my expertise anonymized, and my name will disappear from my knowledge forever

The fact of the matter is I really do love to write. I always said if I wasn't an engineer, I would've been an English teacher. Like many, I feel I could've walked down a lot of paths in life, but writing is one thing I still consider a sacred act. Be it my lab notes, a journal, a text, or even (yes) an email, I believe that writing is one of the most fulfilling things we can spend our time doing. I don't even feel like I should bother explaining it here...in some sense, you get it or you don't. It helps me work out how I'm feeling, what I've learned, what I've yet to learn, and the artifact of it is a snapshot of my mind.  

Think with me for a second...we humans are the most mentally sophisticated creatures that our immediate universe is known to hold. Each one of us is capable of leaving behind -countless- snapshots of our thinking and context at any given time. From a purely selfish perspective, I'd say that I *can't wait* to get older and look back to see moments like this, when I decided a thought was worth capturing. 

SO, that's why I'm writing. Despite the fact that a robot will take it all away from me. Despite the fact that the tech world is increasingly losing its passion for humans who can hold a system in their heads, or who can celebrate humanity and work for its growth. I want to be one of those people. And my hope is that, whether this is read directly by a human or delivered by a robot, maybe it helps someone grow along with me. 

I will say, I don't expect a lot of readership -- and that's fine! This is primarily so I have a place to collect and record things I work on and think are valuable. So I can share it with my family and friends, and one day, myself. If you find it interesting, though, please feel free to reach out :)

*AI be damned, I'm still gonna write!*

## What's It Going to Look Like?

The content will mostly be about embedded systems. Design of boards, programming of microcontrollers (with predominantly C / C++ and hopefully some Rust), book reviews, embedded Linux, etc. But I'm reserving the right to write about music as well if the mood strikes! We'll see. At this point, who can say for sure anyhow?
